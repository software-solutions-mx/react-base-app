## 2. Recommended Page Hierarchy

The site is organized into five buckets. This structure prevents the site from feeling like a political landing page — instead it reads as a compassionate support organization with clear purpose, deep resources, trustworthy safeguards, and transparent governance.

### Bucket A — Mission & Trust

Home, About Us, Mission & Values, How We Help, FAQ, Accessibility, Press / Media Kit

### Bucket B — Support & Guidance

Get Help Now, Resources Hub, Pregnancy Support, Parenting Support, Adoption Information, Emotional Support, Faith and Hope

### Bucket C — Education & Perspective

Understanding Your Options (all sub-pages), The Value of Life (all sub-pages)

### Bucket D — Stories & Community

Testimonials hub, Stories of Choosing Life, Stories After Abortion, Share Your Story, Community Guidelines / Moderation Policy

### Bucket E — Legal & Compliance

Full Legal Center with all policy pages

## 3. Detailed Page-by-Page Guide

---

### Home (`/`)

**Purpose:** Create immediate emotional connection, communicate the organization's heart, orient visitors, and direct them to the right next step — within seconds.

**Main Sections:**

- Hero banner with compassionate headline and warm, hopeful imagery (mother, baby, sunlight, nature)
- Short "Who We Are" overview (2-3 sentences, link to About)
- "You Matter" — a short, direct message aimed at a woman in fear or uncertainty
- Three-path overview: Parenting, Adoption, Understanding Abortion — framed as "we want to help you understand all your options"
- Featured testimonials (2-3 pull quotes with photos or initials)
- Resource spotlight (3-4 highlights: pregnancy center locator, crisis hotline, emotional support, financial help)
- Newsletter signup (optional — must link to privacy policy)
- Footer with full legal links and short disclaimer

**Content Guidance:**

- Hero headline example: "You're not alone. We're here to help you find hope."
- Tone throughout: soft, non-pressuring, non-judgmental
- Credibility element: partner logos or founding year
- Mobile-first layout — crisis resources reachable in one tap from any page

**Calls to Action:**

- "Find a Pregnancy Center Near You"
- "Read Stories of Hope"
- "I Need Help Now" (links to hotlines/resources)
- "Learn About Your Options"

**Footer Disclaimer (appears on every page):**

> This website is for informational and support purposes only. We do not provide medical, legal, or psychiatric services. If you are in immediate danger or crisis, please call 988 or 911.

---

### About Us (`/about`)

**Purpose:** Build trust and credibility. Help visitors understand who is behind this organization, what drives them, and why they can be trusted.

#### Mission & Values (`/about/mission`)

- Clear, concise mission statement
- Core values list: compassion, truth, dignity, hope, non-judgment, support
- Statement of what the organization is and is not: "We are not a medical provider. We are not a political organization. We are a community of people who believe every life matters and every woman deserves support."
- Statement on non-profit, non-political, non-donation-seeking purpose

#### Our Story (`/about/story`)

- Founding narrative — why this organization was created
- Milestone timeline if applicable
- Authentic and personal — this builds emotional trust

#### Our Team (`/about/team`)

- Photos, names, and brief bios of key team members or volunteers
- Credentials disclosed honestly — including non-credentials (e.g., "Jane is a volunteer and a mother of three, not a medical professional.")
- Do not imply medical or clinical authority

#### Organizational Transparency Statement (`/about/transparency`)

- Confirmation of nonprofit status (include EIN if registered 501(c)(3))
- Statement that the site does not accept donations
- Statement that the site does not sell products or services
- No financial relationships with pharmaceutical companies, abortion providers, or anti-abortion political organizations
- Editorial independence statement: who decides what goes on the site and by what standards

> NOTE: The Mission & Values page is where your pro-life position belongs. Present it with the three-part separation: biological facts, ethical perspective, and faith-based belief — each clearly labeled as its own category. This is not weakness — it is what separates a credible advocacy resource from a propaganda site.

### Understanding Your Options (`/options`)

**Purpose:** The most important informational section. Meets women exactly where they are, provides honest and compassionate education, and presents a respectful pro-life perspective without being coercive or medically irresponsible.

#### You Are Not Alone (`/options/you-are-not-alone`)

- Validates feelings of fear, confusion, isolation, and overwhelm
- Reassures that this is a safe, judgment-free space
- Brief overview of what the site offers

#### Continuing Your Pregnancy (`/options/continuing-your-pregnancy`)

- What to expect emotionally and physically during pregnancy
- The support systems available
- Encouragement framed around dignity, strength, and hope
- Disclaimer: "This page contains general educational information only. Please consult a licensed healthcare provider for medical advice."

#### Parenting (`/options/parenting`)

- Honest acknowledgment of challenges alongside the joys
- Available support: financial, social, community, and faith-based
- Stories of women who chose to parent
- Links to parenting resources

#### Adoption (`/options/adoption`)

- Presented as an act of love — not a fallback or a failure
- Types of adoption: open, closed, semi-open
- What the process involves in general terms
- Links to licensed adoption agencies only
- Disclaimer: "We do not represent any adoption agency. All adoption decisions should be made in consultation with licensed legal and adoption professionals."

#### Understanding Abortion (`/options/understanding-abortion`) — HANDLE WITH EXCEPTIONAL CARE

- Present factual information about what abortion is and the types of procedures (medical and surgical) in plain, honest language — without graphic imagery or inflammatory terminology
- Discuss physical, emotional, and psychological aspects that women report — including both those who felt relief and those who experienced distress — citing credible sources where possible
- Distinguish clearly between: (a) medically documented outcomes, (b) ethically contested claims, and (c) faith-based beliefs
- State the organization's perspective explicitly but respectfully: "Our organization believes that abortion ends a human life, and we want women to have full, honest information before making this decision."
- Do not use medically inaccurate claims
- Include emotional support links prominently throughout

**Disclaimer:**

> This page reflects our organization's perspective and is for educational purposes only. It is not medical advice. Please consult a licensed medical professional for guidance specific to your situation.

### The Value of Life (`/value-of-life`)

**Purpose:** Present the pro-life position that human life begins at conception through multiple lenses — biological, philosophical, spiritual, and legal — in a credible, structured, and intellectually honest way.

CRITICAL PRINCIPLE: Every sub-page must clearly label which category of argument it is presenting. Mixing categories without labeling them destroys trust with educated visitors.

#### Biological Perspective (`/value-of-life/biological`)

- What embryology and developmental biology say about the beginning of life
- Cite credible peer-reviewed or textbook sources accurately
- Distinguish clearly: what science describes (a new, genetically distinct human organism begins at fertilization) vs. what is ethically interpreted (whether that organism has moral personhood — this is a philosophical question, not a purely scientific one)
- Disclaimer: "The biological information on this page reflects mainstream embryological science. Moral and ethical interpretations of these facts are the organization's own perspective."

#### Philosophical & Ethical Perspective (`/value-of-life/philosophical`)

- Classic arguments: continuity argument, future-like-ours argument, SLED test (Size, Level of Development, Environment, Degree of Dependency)
- Label explicitly as ethical arguments, not scientific facts
- Acknowledge counterarguments fairly — then explain the organization's response to them
- Avoid strawmanning opposing views

#### Spiritual & Faith Perspective (`/value-of-life/spiritual`)

- Biblical, theological, and other faith-based teachings on the sanctity of life
- Label explicitly as faith content: "This page reflects beliefs rooted in religious and spiritual traditions."
- Welcoming to readers of all faiths and no faith — not exclusive or condemning
- Separated from biological and philosophical pages so non-religious readers can engage without confusion

#### Legal & Historical Context (`/value-of-life/legal-context`)

- Overview of the legal history of abortion in the relevant jurisdiction (e.g., Roe v. Wade, Dobbs v. Jackson)
- Current legal landscape in general terms
- Disclaimer: "This content is for historical and educational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Laws vary by jurisdiction and may change. Please consult a licensed attorney for legal guidance."

**General Resource Disclaimer (appears on all resource pages):**

> The organizations and services listed here are provided for informational purposes only. [Organization name] does not endorse, represent, or guarantee any third-party service. Please verify information directly with each organization.

#### Find Local Help (`/resources/find-local-help`)

- Searchable directory or map tool for pregnancy resource centers by ZIP code or city
- Can integrate with third-party directories (e.g., Care Net, Heartbeat International)

#### Pregnancy Centers (`/resources/pregnancy-centers`)

- What pregnancy resource centers offer (free ultrasounds, counseling, baby supplies, etc.)
- How to find and contact one
- What to expect when you visit

#### Parenting Support (`/resources/parenting-support`)

- WIC, SNAP, Medicaid, and similar government programs (with disclaimer that eligibility varies)
- Nonprofit parenting organizations
- Community and faith-based family support

#### Adoption (`/resources/adoption`)

- Licensed adoption agency directories
- National Adoption Hotline and similar resources
- Legal guidance resources (with disclaimer that this is not legal advice)

#### Emotional & Mental Health Support (`/resources/emotional-support`)

- Post-abortion support organizations (e.g., Rachel's Vineyard, Project Rachel, National Helpline for Abortion Recovery)
- General mental health resources (NAMI, Crisis Text Line, SAMHSA helpline)
- Support groups for women in difficult pregnancies
- Disclaimer: "These resources are not substitutes for professional mental health care. If you are experiencing a mental health crisis, please call 988 or go to your nearest emergency room."

#### Financial Assistance (`/resources/financial-assistance`)

- Government assistance programs
- Nonprofit emergency funds
- Maternity home and housing resources

#### Educational & Career Support (`/resources/educational-career`)

- Resources for completing education (GED, college) and vocational training while pregnant or parenting
- Scholarship programs for mothers
- Childcare assistance for students

#### Faith-Based Support (`/resources/faith-based`)

- Faith communities offering pregnancy support
- Chaplaincy and pastoral counseling resources
- Clearly labeled as faith-based content

#### Hotlines & Crisis Support (`/resources/hotlines`) — LINK FROM EVERY PAGE HEADER OR FOOTER

- National Pregnancy Hotline
- Birthright International

- 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

IMPORTANT: Always include the National Domestic Violence Hotline. Some women facing unexpected pregnancies are in unsafe relationships or experiencing coercion. This resource is essential, and its omission can put visitors at risk.

**Disclaimer:**

> If you are in immediate danger, call 911. These hotlines are staffed by third parties. [Organization name] does not operate these services.

### Testimonials (`/testimonials`)

**Purpose:** Provide real, human stories that help women feel less alone and make more informed, thoughtful decisions. Stories are the most emotionally persuasive content on this type of site — which is also why they carry the highest legal and ethical risk.

#### Stories of Choosing Life (`/testimonials/chose-life`)

- Stories from women who faced unexpected pregnancies, considered abortion, and chose to continue
- Themes: fear turning to strength, unexpected joy, family support, faith, adoption as a loving choice
- Format: photo (or silhouette for privacy), first name or initials, short bio, full story

#### Stories After Abortion (`/testimonials/after-abortion`) — HANDLE WITH EXCEPTIONAL CARE

- Stories from women who chose abortion and later experienced emotional, psychological, relational, or spiritual pain
- Framed as healing journeys — not condemnation
- Include a prominent note that experiences after abortion vary widely
- Do not claim "post-abortion syndrome" as a clinical diagnosis — frame as personal experience, not clinical fact
- Include emotional support resource links prominently throughout this page

#### Share Your Story (`/testimonials/share`)

The submission form must include:

- Name or pseudonym
- Email (if follow-up needed)
- Story text
- Publication preference: public / anonymous / private review only
- Age confirmation checkbox
- Consent & release checkbox
- Link to moderation policy
- Sensitive content warning
- Privacy notice summary

**Editorial Standards:**

- Pre-publication review only — never instant publishing
- No public submissions from minors without verified parental/guardian consent and legal review
- Edit for clarity, length, privacy, and safety
- Verify authenticity before publishing
- Maintain a clear removal request process
- Do not publish medically inaccurate claims as fact

**Testimonial Disclaimer (appears on both story pages):**

> The stories shared here are personal accounts submitted voluntarily by individuals. They represent individual experiences only and are not intended to represent typical outcomes or constitute medical, psychological, or legal advice. [Organization name] makes no representations that any particular experience is common, guaranteed, or likely. Names and identifying details may have been changed or omitted to protect privacy. [Organization name] reserves the right to edit, decline, or remove any submission at its discretion. FTC endorsement guidance applies — stories must not create misleading impressions about typical results.

## 4. Legal Pages & Disclaimer Framework

These pages are not optional. They protect the organization, establish visitor trust, and are required for compliance with applicable law. Have a licensed attorney review every page in this section before the site goes live.

---

### Privacy Policy / Aviso de Privacidad

**Status: REQUIRED**

**Must cover:**

- What data is collected (contact form entries, analytics, cookies, testimonial submissions)
- How it is used and how long it is retained
- Whether stories are published publicly
- How users can request access, deletion, correction, or objection
- Third-party tools and services in use
- Cookie and tracking practices
- International data transfer language

**Jurisdiction notes:**

- Mexico (LFPDPPP): Include a full Aviso de Privacidad and ARCO process per INAI requirements
- EU (GDPR): Add GDPR disclosures including lawful basis and special-category data protections
- California (CCPA/CPRA): Add a California Privacy Rights supplement if applicable
- UK: Add UK GDPR language if serving UK users

SPECIAL NOTE FOR THIS SITE'S AUDIENCE: Women seeking sensitive health-related information have a heightened expectation of privacy. Your policy should explicitly state that personal data is never sold, and should address cooperation with law enforcement as narrowly as possible under applicable law. This is a serious trust issue for your audience.

---

### Terms of Use

**Status: REQUIRED**

**Must cover:**

- Permitted and prohibited use
- No reliance on site content as professional advice of any kind

## 6. Content Best Practices

These principles should guide every page, every paragraph, and every word written for this site.

---

### 1. Compassion Before Conviction

Every page should first make the reader feel seen, safe, and valued as a person — before presenting any perspective or argument. A woman in crisis will close the tab the moment she feels judged. Lead always with empathy. Write to frightened, overwhelmed, ashamed, pressured, or isolated visitors as people who need dignity and clarity — not as subjects of a moral argument.

---

### 2. Rigorously Separate Fact, Ethics, and Faith — With Clear Labels

Never blend these three categories without labeling them. Use explicit labels throughout:

- Biological fact: "A new, unique human genome is formed at fertilization." (Cite source.)
- Ethical view: "Based on this biological reality, we hold the ethical conviction that human life deserves protection from its earliest stage."
- Faith claim: "We believe every life is a sacred gift." (If applicable.)

This is not weakness — it is what separates a credible advocacy resource from a propaganda site. Educated visitors will immediately recognize and distrust unlabeled category mixing.

---

### 3. Avoid Inflammatory Language — Everywhere

Do not use terms like "baby killer," "murderer," or similar language anywhere on the site. Do not use graphic imagery. Do not use emotionally manipulative or fear-based content designed to shame or frighten visitors. When describing the pro-choice perspective or experiences of women who felt relief after abortion, represent those views accurately. These tactics undermine credibility, harm vulnerable readers, and expose the organization to reputational and legal risk.

---

### 4. Medical Claims Must Be Accurate and Carefully Framed

Do not make unsupported medical claims. Do not claim that abortion causes breast cancer, permanent infertility, or clinical mental illness unless you are citing peer-reviewed sources and framing them responsibly. Unsupported or overstated medical claims expose the organization to serious legal and reputational harm — and most importantly, they harm the women the site is trying to serve.

Safe framing:

- YES: "Some women report..."
- YES: "This organization believes..."
- YES: "According to [reliable source]..."
- YES: "Personal experiences differ..."
- NO: "Abortion always causes..."
- NO: "Every woman regrets it."
- NO: "Science proves only one moral conclusion."

---

### 5. Never Make Guarantees — Offer Support Instead

Never guarantee that choosing life will lead to a specific outcome. Never say "you'll be happy" or "your partner will stay." Instead: "Many women find that accessing the resources and support available here helps them navigate parenting with confidence and hope."

---

### 6. Every Argument Page Must End With a Path to Support

Never leave a reader with only a moral argument and no place to go. Every page presenting a perspective or educational content should end with a resource link, a hotline reference, or a "you are not alone" message. The goal is to inform and support — not to argue and abandon.

---

### 7. Minimize Data Collection

Only collect what you truly need. In contact and submission forms: name or pseudonym, email if follow-up is needed, story text, publication preference, consent checkbox. Do not casually collect detailed health histories. Given the sensitive nature of your audience, data minimization is both a sound privacy practice and an ethical commitment to the women using the site.

---

### 8. Moderate Testimonials Aggressively — Pre-Publication Only

This topic attracts harassment, fabricated stories, and attempts to provoke conflict. Use pre-publication review only — never instant publishing. Establish written editorial standards. Have a legal advisor review the testimonial workflow before launch. Allow anonymous or first-name-only publication as the default. Maintain and honor a removal request process.

---

### 9. Cite Sources for Substantive Claims

Any scientific, medical, or psychological claim should be linked to a credible, verifiable source (peer-reviewed journal, government health agency, mainstream medical textbook). This builds credibility and protects against accusations of misinformation. Where you rely on contested or preliminary research, say so explicitly — present it as one source among several, not as settled consensus.

---

### 10. Steelman Opposing Views — Then Present Your Own

When describing the pro-choice position or the experiences of women who felt relief after abortion, represent those views accurately before explaining your own position. Presenting the strongest version of an opposing argument — then explaining your response — makes your own arguments more persuasive and more trustworthy. Strawmanning opposing views signals intellectual weakness and alienates thoughtful readers.

---

### 11. Accessibility, Readability, and Language

- Target a 7th-8th grade reading level for maximum accessibility
- Use short paragraphs; avoid jargon
- If serving multilingual communities, offer content in Spanish or other relevant languages
- Design mobile-first — many women in crisis will access this site on a smartphone, possibly in private browsing mode
- Crisis hotlines and help resources must be reachable in one tap from any page
- All images should have descriptive alt text
